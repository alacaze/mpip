Masters of Clinical Pharmacy 2020

Adam La Caze

28 November 2019

Introduction

  • We have a new MClinPharm program available from 2020

  • The new MClinPharm is the same size as the “old” GradDipClinPharm

  • No new enrolments to the GradDipClinPharm or old MClinPharm (but current students can complete within their expected time frame)

Drivers

  1. Changes in how Level 9 Masters Degrees were defined within the Australian Qualifications Framework, and

  2. Recognition that the year-long research project within the MClinPharm program did not meet the needs of all students.

Therapeutics

  • PHRM7031 (S1), PHRM7032 (S2), PHRM7033 (S2)
  • PHRM7031 and PHRM7032 are prerequisites for PHRM7033

Credit arrangements

  • Completion of PHRM7030 provides credit for PHRM7031 and PHRM7033
  • Completion of PHRM7060 provides credit for PHRM7032 and PHRM7033

Advanced Practice Project (PHRM7095, 2 units, Semester 2)

  • Replaces PHRM7071/2 (6 unit, year-long subject) and the elective placements or biostatistics subject

  • The project can be a quality improvement project, management project or a small research project

  • Implementation of PHRM7021 proposal or PHRM7051 business case proposal (or something new)

  • Getting the scale right will be important

Additional changes

  • Better align activities and recognition of the work that pharmacists do as part of the SHPA Foundation Residency Program

  • The aim is to have the flexibility to meet the needs to pharmacists who have completed, or are completing, a residency program and those who are not

  • To incorporate activities throughout the program related to the development of an Advanced Pharmacy Practice portfolio

What does this mean for you?

  • If you are in the GCClinPharm or GDipClinPharm, you can articulate to the new MClinPharm

  • If you have completed a course that is in the MClinPharm within the last couple of years you will receive credit for that course

  • Credit arrangements for the therapeutics courses apply (as above)

  • There are rules about how long you can claim credit for a course as part of a Masters program (UQ PPL 3.50.03b, 5.5.8.2)

What do I need to do?

  • If you wish to stay in your current program and complete it within the expected timeframe, there is no further action

  • If you wish to change from your current program to the new #16 Master of Clinical Pharmacy program, you must submit a program change via mySI-net.

Please note: Program changes for Semester 1, 2020 open in SI-net on 2 January 2020. Once open you will be able to submit to change your program in SI-net.
