Should pharmacists sell homeopathy?

Adam La Caze

25 September 2019

Debate

verb:

argue about (a subject), especially in a formal manner

Topics

  1. Pharmacists should not recommend homeopathy
  2. Pharmacists should sell homeopathy
  3. Expecting more from pharmacists when they sell complementary medicines than health stores imposes unfair costs on pharmacies.
  4. Stocking homeopathy in a pharmacy provides an implicit recommendation that it is effective

Code of Ethics (Australia)

Integrity Principle 1 (h)

A pharmacist will only purchase, supply or promote any medicine, complementary medicine, herbal remedy or other healthcare product where there is credible evidence of efficacy and that benefit of use outweighs the risk

Singapore Pharmacy Council: Code of ethics (2015)

Pledge:

  • Practise my profession with honesty, integrity and compassion;
  • Honour traditions and embrace advancements in my profession;
  • Abide by the governing laws and Code of Ethics;
  • Respect and keep in confidence patient information;
  • Maintain a high standard of professional competence through life long learning;
  • Always place patient’s interests first, and treat them equally;
  • Collaborate with other healthcare colleagues to achieve the desired treatment outcomes;
  • Impart my knowledge, experience and skills to nurture future pharmacists;
  • Strive to provide high quality and cost-effective health services and products;
  • Translate scientific advances into better healthcare.
